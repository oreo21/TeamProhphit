    • Students may apply for as many Advanced Placement courses as they are eligible for, as determined by the following criteria:

    1. Any student who meets the departmental requirements for an individual Advanced Placement course will be eligible for that course regardless of the student's overall average.
    2. Students who wish to take two Advanced Placement classes must have a MINIMUM overall average of 88%.
    3. Students who wish to take three Advanced Placement classes must have a MINIMUM overall average of 93%.
    4. Students who wish to take four Advanced Placement classes must have a MINIMUM overall average of 95%.

    # Course Title Course Code Prereqs
    1 AP Biology SBS43X & SBS44X Full-year, double-period course. Juniors & Seniors. By application. Minimum biology and science average of 93% required. Students much choose as a prerequisite or co-requisite from either: A. Biological Lab Techniques, Genetics Research, Vertebrate Zoology, Molecular Genetics, Human Medical Genetics, Human Disease, Neurobiology or B. The Spring term of the Freshman Research Advanced Topics class or the full year Freshman Advanced Topics class (prerequisites only). Course code for the Fall semester of Freshman Advanced Topics is SBS21QT and the course code for the Spring semester for Freshman Advanced Topics is SBS22QT.
    2 AP Environmental Science SWS21X & SWS22X Full-year course. Juniors & Seniors. By application. Minimum biology and science average of 88% required. One co-requisite or prerequisite from among the following courses is required: Meteorology, Geology, Oceanography, or Urban Ecology.

    5 AP Computer Science MKS21X & MKS22X This is a full-year course. Students who enroll in the fall will automatically be programmed for the spring. Students will be considered for this course if they have received a grade of at least 92% in MKS21: Introduction to Computer Science.

    13 AP Calculus AB MCS21X & MCS22X This is a full year course and cannot be dropped mid-year. A minimum Math average of 85% and a minimum first semester grade of 85% in Pre-calculus or 90% in Advanced Algebra are required. Pre-calculus is a year-long course which contains the skills in operations with functions and graphing that are essential for success in calculus.
    14 AP Calculus BC MCS43X & MCS44X This is a full year course and cannot be dropped mid-year. The pre or co-requisite is Pre-calculus, a year long course which contains the skills in operations with functions and graphing that are essential for success in calculus. Pre-calculus and calculus may be studied concurrently since topics are taught in pre-calculus earlier in the year than when they are needed in calculus. Students who took Honors Pre-calculus must have a minimum math average of 94% and a first semester Honors Pre-calculus grade of 90% or higher. Students who took regular Pre-calculus must have a minimum math average of 94% and a first semester Pre-calculus grade of 94% or higher. Strong Math students may opt to take Honors Pre-Calculus as a co-requisite. Pre-calculus and calculus can be studied concurrently since topics are taught in pre-calculus earlier in the year than when they are needed in calculus. Students who wish to double up in this manner must have a minimum 95% average in mathematics and a first semester Honors Algebra 2/Trigonometry grade of 95% or higher.
    15 AP Statistics MSS21X & MSS22X This course is open to juniors with a math average of 85% and to sophomores who have a math average of at least 94% and who will have completed Algebra 2 before the course begins. An interest in current events, such as public opinions and trends in the sciences and sports would be helpful.
    16 AP Music Theory UUS21X & UUS22X The student who has not taken Music Appreciation or does not play in a Stuyvesant band or orchestra will be required to take a short evaluation exam or obtain Department Chairperson approval. The AP Exam in Music Theory, offered in May, is strongly recommended. For students who do not take the AP test, there will be an alternative classroom test.
    17 AP Physics B SPS21X & SPS22X AP Physics B is a double-period, one-year course that is scheduled as AP Physics 1 in the fall term and AP Physics 2 in the spring term. If you enroll in AP Physics 1 in the fall term, you will automatically be programmed for AP Physics 2 in the spring term. Students must commit themselves to completing the entire year and take the AP exam (there are no exceptions).
    18 AP Physics C SPS43X & SPS44X Prerequisite: Physics with a minimum overall average of 92%, a minimum Physics average of 94% and the completion of AP Calculus AB (94% average) or AP Calculus BC (92% average). Students may take AP Physics C concurrently with AP Calculus BC, provided the student meets the criteria of both departments. This is a one-year course. If you enroll in the fall you will automatically be enrolled for the second semester in the spring. Students must commit themselves to completing the entire year and take the AP exam (there are no exceptions).
